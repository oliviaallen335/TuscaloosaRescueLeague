Frequently asked questions

How do I adopt a pet?

Browse our catalog of available animals, find one you're interested in, and submit an adoption application. Our team will review your application and reach out to schedule a meet-and-greet. Once approved, you'll complete the adoption paperwork and pay any adoption fee.

What are the adoption fees?

Adoption fees vary by animal and help cover vaccinations, spay/neuter, microchipping, and basic care. The fee for each animal is listed in their profile. Fees are due when you finalize the adoption.

What is included when I adopt?

Each adopted pet goes home with a packet that includes health records, vaccination history, microchip info, and care guidelines. See our Adoption info page for the full checklist.

Can I meet an animal before applying?

Yes. After you submit an application, we'll contact you to schedule a visit. You can meet the animal and ask our staff any questions before making a final decision.

How long does the adoption process take?

Applications are typically reviewed within 1–3 business days. Once approved, you can usually complete the adoption within a few days, depending on scheduling.

What if the pet doesn't work out?

We understand that sometimes a placement isn't the right fit. Contact us as soon as possible. We ask that you return the animal to us so we can find them a better match. Adoption fees are generally non-refundable.

Do you adopt to out-of-state applicants?

Policies vary. Please include your location in your application, and we'll let you know if we can accommodate an adoption to your area.

How can I support the agency if I'm not ready to adopt?

You can foster animals, donate supplies, volunteer, or make a monetary donation. Check our Events page for upcoming fundraisers and volunteer opportunities.
